# StockStory — Product Maturity Evaluation

This report classifies StockStory's current product maturity and outlines its transformation into a **Personal Investor Operating System**.

---

## 1. Product Classification

We classify StockStory as a **Personal Investor Operating System (Investor OS)**.

### Why this classification?
* **Research Tool (Legacy)**: In its early phases, StockStory was a research tool. It displayed static metrics and charts, but lacked personalization or active alert systems.
* **Investor Platform (Intermediate)**: As it expanded to cover 505 real securities with live Postgres synchronization and backend APIs, it became an investor platform. However, it still required the user to manually seek out data.
* **Investor Operating System (Current)**: With the addition of the Watchlist Suite, Daily Briefing alerts, Portfolio V2 risk auditing, and Investor Memory, StockStory has transformed into an active operating system. The system now:
  * Tracks user-specific portfolios and watchlists.
  * Dynamically computes personalized risks (e.g. concentration risk, sector drift).
  * Automatically generates customized briefs and alerts.
  * Acts as an active daily workspace for capital allocation.

---

## 2. Product Maturity Scorecard

```
Evolutionary Stages:
[Stage 1] Research Tool (Static data & charts) ── Completed
[Stage 2] Investor Platform (Live APIs, 505 stocks) ── Completed
[Stage 3] Investor Operating System (Personalization, Alerts, Memory) ── ACTIVE
```

* **Core Difference**: Traditional tools display data. An Investor OS **actively audits and guides** the investor's daily workflow, translating numbers into plain-English stories.
