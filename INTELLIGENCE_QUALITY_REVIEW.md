# StockStory — Intelligence Quality Review

This review audits the quality, depth, and linguistic variety of the dynamic text generated by StockStory's intelligence engines. It covers **Company Narratives**, **Market Briefs**, **Sector Briefs**, and **Portfolio Briefs**.

---

## Audit of Narrative Outputs

### 1. Company Narratives (narrative50, narrative100, narrative250)
* **Design**: The system generates text in three lengths to fit different layout sizes.
* **Findings**:
  * **Repetition**: The narrative generator uses a template-based system that combines dynamic words (e.g. symbol names, score numbers) with pre-written sentences. For example, similar stocks like `TCS` and `INFY` often output:
    > *"INFY represents a standard corporate profile with normal factor exposure limits. Risk and valuation levels are in neutral territory."*
    > *"TCS represents a standard corporate profile with normal factor exposure limits. Risk and valuation levels are in neutral territory."*
    This repetition stands out when comparing multiple stocks within the same sector.
  * **Generic Wording**: The system defaults to standard sentences like *"demonstrating balanced factor metrics, placing it in a stable consolidation regime"* whenever a stock's factors fall between 40 and 60.
  * **Weak Insights**: While the engine lists specific technical values (e.g., RSI is 55, MACD histogram is positive), it rarely explains the *why* (e.g., linking a high MACD histogram to specific volume spikes or market announcements).

### 2. Market Briefs
* **Design**: Serves as the headline summary on the Dashboard ("Today's Brief"), designed to give the investor a snapshot of overall market health.
* **Findings**:
  * **Repetition**: The market brief heavily repeats templates based on the ratio of advancing to declining stocks. If the market is moderately positive, the brief consistently outputs: *"Moderate upward trend strength detected across indices. Sector rotation indicates active capital deployment."*
  * **Generic Wording**: The brief uses high-level phrases like *"stable macroeconomic conditions"* and *"market-wide consolidation"* instead of identifying the specific index movers or macroeconomic releases (e.g., RBI rate decisions, inflation numbers) that caused the movement.

### 3. Sector Briefs
* **Design**: Displayed in the Sector Explorer, describing sector momentum, leadership, and rotation signals (e.g., BULL, BEAR, ACCUMULATION).
* **Findings**:
  * **Repetition**: When multiple sectors share the same classification (e.g., Banking and Technology are both "Steady"), their briefs use identical structures:
    > *"Sector strength is steady with moderate momentum and moderate risk. The rotation signal is HOLD."*
  * **Weak Insights**: The briefs describe the average factor score of the sector but fail to list which specific stock is leading the trend or dragging it down (e.g., noting that a rally in the banking sector is led by `HDFCBANK` rather than regional banks).

### 4. Portfolio Briefs
* **Design**: Created dynamically when a user inputs portfolio weights, summarizing diversification status and overall factor positioning.
* **Findings**:
  * **Repetition**: The portfolio brief uses a fixed template:
    > *"The portfolio is [diversificationStatus] with focus in the [maxSector] sector. Overall quality ([quality]) and risk ([risk]) factor exposure profiles reflect [posture]..."*
  * **Generic Wording**: The use of fixed qualifiers like "high stability" and "momentum consolidation headwinds" makes every user portfolio sound identical, whether it is highly aggressive or defensive.

## Critique Summary & Improvement Matrix

| Surface | Major Issue | Severity | Visual / UX Impact | Proposed Solution |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Company Narratives** | Template repetition | Medium | High when comparing stocks | Implement a synonym-rotation engine and dynamic paragraph transitions. |
| **Market Briefs** | Lack of real-world catalysts | Low | Medium | Pull recent market news headings directly into the brief generator. |
| **Sector Briefs** | No constituent awareness | Medium | Low | Dynamically query and list top-contributing and bottom-contributing symbols. |
| **Portfolio Briefs** | Rigid sentence structures | Low | Medium | Write dedicated paragraph templates based on specific exposure ratios. |

---

## Action Plan for Future Sprints

1. **Linguistic Variance (Synonym Matrices)**: Update the template system to randomly select from a list of synonyms for standard phrases (e.g. replace "stable consolidation regime" with "balanced valuation range" or "steady trading channel").
2. **Context-Aware Catalysts**: Modify the `ExplanationEngine` to pull specific metrics into sentences (e.g., instead of saying "high business quality," write "high business quality, driven by an exceptional ROE of 24%").
3. **Dynamic Constituent Highlights**: Update sector briefs to query the database for the strongest stock in the sector and output: *"Led by [Symbol] with a momentum score of [Score]"*.
